## 1. Quality Policy

Sai Life Sciences Pvt. Ltd. is committed to delivering Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients and Intermediates of consistent quality, in compliance with applicable regulatory requirements (US FDA cGMP, EU GMP Part II, ICH Q7, Schedule M India, WHO PQ), under a Pharmaceutical Quality System aligned with **ICH Q10**.

Our policy commitments:

1. **Patient safety first** in every quality decision
2. **Data integrity by design** — ALCOA+ enforced at system level
3. **Continuous improvement** through risk-based investigation and CAPA closure
4. **Compliance with regulator expectations** in all markets we serve
5. **People-first** — competence built through training, succession, and culture

## 2. Scope of Quality Management System

Applies to:
- API and intermediate manufacturing across Hyderabad and Bidar sites
- Quality Control laboratories (chemical and microbiology)
- Warehouse operations (raw materials, intermediates, APIs)
- Contract giver activities for outsourced testing and packaging

Excludes:
- Clinical Research operations (governed by separate CRO QMS)
- Discovery research (governed by R&D QMS)

## 4. Pharmaceutical Quality System Elements (ICH Q10)

### 4.1 Process Performance and Product Quality Monitoring System
Continuous monitoring of CPP (Critical Process Parameters) and CQA (Critical Quality Attributes) via in-process controls and trend reviews. Periodic Product Quality Review per product per year.

### 4.2 Corrective and Preventive Action System
All deviations, complaints, OOS results, and audit findings flow to CAPA. CAPA closure requires effectiveness verification. Recurring root cause triggers extended investigation.

### 4.3 Change Management System
All changes affecting product quality, process, equipment, or controlled documents are routed through Change Control. Risk-based categorisation determines approval level and notification obligations to customers and regulators.

### 4.4 Management Review of Process Performance and Product Quality
Quarterly Management Review meeting. Inputs:
- KPI scorecard (deviation rate, CAPA cycle time, complaint rate, batch failure rate)
- Audit findings (internal, customer, regulator)
- Training compliance
- Resource adequacy

Outputs: documented decisions and action items tracked to closure.

### 4.5 Knowledge Management
Process and product knowledge captured in:
- Master Batch Records (current revision controlled)
- Development reports
- Risk assessment files (ICH Q9)
- Tech transfer dossiers

### 4.6 Quality Risk Management (ICH Q9)
Risk-based approach to:
- Equipment and computerized system validation
- Supplier qualification
- Change control impact assessment
- Deviation classification
